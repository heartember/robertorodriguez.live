Working Together

I work with people doing meaningful work who want to expand their capacity—personally and collectively— for complexity, conflict, and transformation.

Whether you’re navigating organizational change, relational rupture, leadership challenges, or movement dynamics, we’ll build the skills and practices to meet what’s here—without abandoning yourself or others.

My work draws from somatic practice, systems thinking, conflict transformation, and movement history. Together we focus on real-world conditions: limited time, high stakes, mixed incentives, and human nervous systems.

Approach

A multi-level practice of relational wholeness: self, other, system.

Relational wholeness is the ability to stay connected to yourself, to others, and to the larger system— especially when conditions are challenging. We work at multiple levels:

  • Somatic: what your nervous system is doing and why it matters
  • Relational: communication, boundaries, repair, and trust
  • Systemic: power, history, institutions, incentives, and culture

My orientation is grounded, practical, and anti-performative. We focus on what works in real relationships— not what sounds good.
